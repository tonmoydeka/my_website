About Me

Hi! Thanks for visiting my website. I am Tonmoy Deka and I am interested in the study of planetary atmospheres, as they play a pivotal role in shaping a planet's characteristics. A compelling example is the contrast between Earth and Venusâ€”despite their similar mass and radius, their surface conditions are drastically different. Venus, with a surface temperature over 450 K hotter and an atmospheric pressure nearly 90 times that of Earth, owes these extremes entirely to the differences in atmospheric composition and dynamics. My current focus is on understanding exoplanetary atmospheres through a combination of modeling and observational techniques. By studying these distant worlds, we can gain critical insights into the processes that shape atmospheric diversity, enhance our understanding of planet formation, and explore the potential for habitability in other planetary systems..

Projects

  • Project 1: Developing an Exoplanet Atmospheric Retrieval Algorithm.

    This project focuses on creating a robust algorithm for the retrieval of atmospheric parameters of exoplanets. The main objectives include:

    • 1. Developing a forward model that can do transmission, emission, and reflection spectra simulations.
    • 2. Retrievals using Bayesian nested sampling algorithms.
    • 3. Collaborating with other group members to integrate ML retrieval into the code.
